Does everyone have access to electricity?

Does everyone have access to electricity?

To the left is a visualisation of the percentage energy access of countries in the year 2019. The large majority of countries around the world are able to provide energy access to almost 100% of their residents. Some notable regions with low energy access percentages include the Africa and Asia regions. Specifically, South Sudan, Chad and the Central African Republic have energy access percentages of 6.7%, 8.4% and 14.3% respectively.

Providing "access to affordable, reliable, sustainable and modern energy for all" is the 7th sustainable developement goal set out by the United Nations. While almost 90% of the world's population have access to electricity, providing adaquate service and infrastructure to those in predominately rural locations will prove to be a challenge heading into the future. Which countries consume the most energy per person?

The visualisation to the left depicts the annual energy consumption per capita categorised by country. The total energy consumption per capita is measured by a logarthmic scale using units of kWh.

For reference, on kWh is the equivalent of running the microwave for one hour straight, or charging a phone for 60 hours. What type of primary energy sources are being used?

Below is a visualisation of the increase in global energy consumption by primary energy source type. From the beginning of the data in 1800, traditional biomass (e.g. wood, charcoal and leaves) was the main source of energy and continues to be a significant portion of the energy mix in developing countries. Today, a large percentage of energy consumed is derived from coal, gas and oil, while newer emerging technologies such as nuclear power, hydropower, solar power and biofuels occupy a small proportion of the mix.

What type of renewable energy sources are being used?

The consumption of energy derived from sources that include coal, gas and oil and the associated emmission of greenhouse gases is a key factor in the global climate change issue. The need to "take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts" is the 13th sustainable development goal set out by the United Nations (UN). According to the UN, 2019 was the secondest warmest year ever recorded and the amount of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere reached an all time high. Do wealthier countries consume more energy per person?

The following scatterplot indicates that there is a general correlation between the annual energy consumption per person and the GDP per capita of a country. The GDP per capita of each country has been standardised using the international dollar. For a given country, one international dollar would purchase an equivalent amount of goods and services that one U.S dollar could obtain in the United States of America. The countries have further been categorised based on their continent. The legend on the right can be used as a filter to view a specific continent.
