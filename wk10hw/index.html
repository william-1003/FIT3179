Hidden text

The following scatterplot indicates that there is a general correlation between the annual energy consumption per person and the GDP per capita of a country. The GDP per capita of each country has been standardised using the international dollar. For a given country, one international dollar would purchase an equivalent amount of goods and services that one U.S dollar could obtain in the United States of America. Furthermore, the total energy consumption per capita is measured in units of kWh. For reference, one kWh is the equivalent of running the microwave for one hour straight, or charging a phone for 60 hours.
